Insights with stratergic analysis and data intelligence.

In my recent project, DreamHomez, I leveraged over 20 years' worth of real estate data to conduct a comprehensive analysis aimed at optimizing business strategies and driving growth. The project involved meticulous data cleaning and integration using industry-leading tools such as Tableau Prep Builder and Excel, culminating in insightful visualizations crafted in Tableau.

One of the key insights gleaned from the visualization was the concerning trajectory of DreamHomez's year-over-year growth, particularly within the house segment, which serves as a cornerstone of the company's revenue. Despite a consistent market demand indicating willingness to pay above assessed values, the company's sales volumes failed to fully capitalize on this opportunity. This discovery underscored the necessity for strategic realignment to leverage potential revenue gains.

Problem Statement: Despite a growing demand for various property types, DreamHomez has experienced a drop in total sales and profits, indicating the need for strategic adjustments in sales and marketing approaches.

Objective: Leveraging data insights to formulate targeted strategies that enhance sales and profit margins for DreamHomez.

DreamHomez's Year-over-Year Growth Challenges

The charts present a concerning trajectory for DreamHomez, with year-over-year growth taking a turbulent descent, especially within the house segment—a cornerstone of the company's revenue and profit engine.

The Bigger, the Better: Sales Rise with Property Size

This scatter plot with regression lines reveals a clear positive correlation between property size and sales amount within DreamHomez's real estate portfolio. The consistent upward trend suggests that larger properties tend to sell for higher prices.

Bigger Spaces, Bigger Profits: The Size-Profit Correlation

The scatter plot showcases a clear trend: as property sizes increase, gross profits rise. This correlation indicates that DreamHomez’s larger properties are delivering substantial profit margins.

Matching Portfolios to Market Demand Curves

The histogram highlights a concentration of market demand in specific property size ranges, with a peak in properties around 50 to 120 square meters. DreamHomez's portfolio strategy must balance this clear consumer preference with the profitability and revenue potential of various property sizes to optimize growth and financial performance.

Optimal Pricing for Targeting the Market's Sweet Spot

The histogram reveals a distinct peak in property transactions within the price range of $5000 to $6000 per square meter, indicating a sweet spot in market pricing. DreamHomez can harness this insight to strategically price properties, tapping into the most active segment of the market to maximize sales volume and drive sustainable growth.

The comprehensive analysis has revealed that DreamHomez's sales slump can be addressed by refocusing business strategy on the most profitable towns and aligning our property offerings with market demands in terms of type, size, and price point.

Key Findings and Recommendations:

1. Prioritize Top Performers: Intensify sales initiatives in the top 20% of towns that are the main contributors to the bottom line, particularly emphasizing the house segment.

2. Tailor Property Dimensions: Align product offerings with the preferred property sizes, focusing on 100-120 square meters for houses and 50-100 square meters for apartments, catering to the most sought-after market segments.

3. Refine Pricing Approach: Adjust the pricing strategies to hit the sweet spot of $5000 - $6000 per square meter, capturing the core of the sales volume.

4. Performance Monitoring: Roll out a monthly performance tracking system to ensure all sales and marketing efforts are data-driven, responsive, and optimized for profit and growth.


Target and Impact:

Implementing these targeted strategies is expected to more effectively tap into current demands, thereby attracting new customers and driving overall business growth.
