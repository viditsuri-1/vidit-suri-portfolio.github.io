Insights with stratergic analysis and data intelligence.

Key Activities:


1. Data Collection and Preprocessing:

The project began with the acquisition of COVID-19 data from COVID-19 Data Repository by the Center for Systems Science and Engineering (CSSE) at Johns Hopkins University. This raw data was cleaned to ensure consistency and accuracy. Preprocessing tasks included handling missing values, standardizing data formats, and aggregating information at the desired granularity (e.g., country-level or province/state-level).

You can check out the database here.


2. Statistical Analysis:

Using Pandas, I conducted a range of statistical analyses to uncover patterns and trends in the COVID-19 data. This included computing summary statistics such as total confirmed cases, deaths, recovered cases, and active cases by country. Additionally, advanced analytics techniques were employed to identify correlations, growth rates, and hotspots of infection.


3. Visualization and Interpretation:

Data visualization played a crucial role in conveying insights from the analysis effectively. Matplotlib and Seaborn were utilized to create visually appealing plots and charts that summarized key findings. These visualizations included country-wise comparisons of COVID-19 metrics, time series plots illustrating the progression of the pandemic, and geographical representations of regional variations. Interpretation of the visualizations helped in grasping the magnitude of the pandemic's impact.

Learnings:


1. Data Manipulation with Pandas:

Through this project, I enhanced my proficiency in data manipulation techniques using Pandas. This included tasks such as data cleaning, filtering, aggregation, and merging to prepare the dataset for analysis.


2. Statistical Analysis Techniques:

I gained hands-on experience in applying various statistical analysis techniques to extract meaningful insights from COVID-19 data. This included descriptive statistics, correlation analysis, time series analysis, and outlier detection methods.


3. Data Visualization with Matplotlib:

The project provided valuable experience in data visualization using Matplotlib. I learned how to create a diverse range of plots and charts to effectively communicate complex data trends and patterns to diverse audiences


4. Understanding Public Health Data:

Analyzing COVID-19 data deepened my understanding of public health issues and epidemiological trends. It highlighted the importance of timely and accurate data in addressing global health challenges.
